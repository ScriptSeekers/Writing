Prem Ki Kavita
A quiet, poetic metaphor for love that is expressed through absence, pauses, and interpretation—where meaning depends on how deeply the reader feels.
Priy likhakar niche likh du naam tumhara, kuch jagah beech me chhod, niche likh dun sada tumhara. Likha beech me kya, yeh tumko padhna hai, kagaz par man ki bhasha ka artha samajhna hai. Jo bhi artha nikalogi tum, wah mujhko swikar; jhuke adhar, jhuke nayan, maun adhar ya kora kagaz — artha sabhi ka pyar.
Love & Fight
A blunt reminder that love without courage is just comfort—real love demands effort, resistance, and the willingness to stand your ground.
If you're a lover, you gotta be a fighter. Because if you don't fight for your love, what kind of love do you have?
Everything Is Yours
Total emotional surrender in its purest form—short, direct, and dangerously absolute.
You are the love of my life. Everything I have. And everything I am. Is yours.
Can I
A gentle request for forever, built on presence, patience, and emotional safety rather than grand promises.
Can I hold your hand, not just today, but through every storm and sunrise? & Can I be the one who listens to your silence and still understands? Can I stay when the world feels too heavy, and remind you how light love can feel? Can I love you in all the little ways in every glance, every word, every heartbeat- for as long as forever remembers us?
4, 3, 2, 1
A minimalist expression of timeless love—reducing time itself to a single constant: everyday.
I can love you for 4 days Summer day, Winter day, Rainy day, Autumn day. May be 3 days Yesterday, Today, Tomorrow. No it should be 2 times Day & Night. May be 1 day Everyday.
