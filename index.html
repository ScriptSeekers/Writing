Ishq Ki Aag
A confession of love so intense it turns devotion into self-destruction—where the heart worships instead of choosing, and love burns more than it heals.
Maine pucha apne Ishq se, "Kya main kar sakta hoon tumhari fikar?" Ishq ne dhadkan chhoo kar kaha, "Jo tumhara dil chahe, wahi sachkar." Dil ne cheekh kar kaha, "Main tumhara hona chahta hoon… Poora, bina kisi bhi shart ke." Par main dil de na saka, Kyuki yeh bechaara dil Tumhari har ada, har nazakat ko Bhagwan ki tarah chahne laga hai. Ab halat yeh hai Mera dil bhi Ishq ki tarah Tum par hi jalta, Tum par hi pighalta, Tum par hi bikhar raha hai. Ki chandi sona ek taraf, aur tera hona ek taraf. Ek taraf teri aankhen, jaadu tona ek taraf.
Prem Ki Kavita
A quiet, poetic metaphor for love that is expressed through absence, pauses, and interpretation—where meaning depends on how deeply the reader feels.
Priy likhakar niche likh du naam tumhara, kuch jagah beech me chhod, niche likh dun sada tumhara. Likha beech me kya, yeh tumko padhna hai, kagaz par man ki bhasha ka artha samajhna hai. Jo bhi artha nikalogi tum, wah mujhko swikar; jhuke adhar, jhuke nayan, maun adhar ya kora kagaz — artha sabhi ka pyar.

Dono Ke Beech
A story of mutual feelings trapped in silence, where both hearts know the truth but fear timing more than regret.
Na tu bola, na main, par har lafz ke beech kuch tha. Hawa bhi jaanti thi, ke khamoshi mein ishq sa kuch tha. Tere dekhne ka andaaz, aur mera nazar jhuka lena, ye dono adhoori baatein, par dil ka keh jaana. Kisi ne poocha, “kuch chal raha hai?” hum dono muskura diye, ek jaise jazbaat the dono ke, bas alfaaz kahaan diye? Tu bhi samajhta tha, main bhi mehsoos karti thi, par dono ne khud se kaha, “abhi waqt nahi, bas yehi sahi.” Aur waqt guzarta gaya, par wo ehsaas wahi tha na izhaar hua, na inkaar, par pyaar… dono ke dilon mein wahi tha. ❤️
The First Time I Saw Her
An internal battle between hope and restraint—about falling for someone slowly, honestly, and not knowing whether to risk friendship for truth.
The first time I saw her, the world slowed down for a breath. I was on the field sweat, dust, and nightlight and there she stood, in a white jacket and black jeans, composed, distant, whispering to her phone, as if the universe bowed quietly to her calm. I don't know why, but something inside me stirred that day a pull, a quiet ache. She felt like a story I could never belong to, someone far beyond my reach, a league I could only dream of entering. Four years slipped by like passing clouds, and now, she talks to me. Sometimes we walk, side by side, under evening skies, and I swear, she feels almost like my type maybe even more. I've never lied to her, not once my heart's been an open page. But hers? I can't read it yet. And now I stand here, torn should I keep this fragile friendship, or risk it all, and let love take its chance?

Hamesha
A grounded take on lifelong friendship—less about promises, more about consistency, presence, and remembering each other despite life.
Forever dosti koi vaada nahi, ek choti si zimmedaari hoti hai. Hum chahe pehli priority na bane, par ek priority zaroor hote hain. Zindagi sabko busy karti hai, raaste alag-alag mod leti hain. Phir bhi asli dosti tab dikhti hai jab hum yaad rakhte hain ki koi humara bhi hai. Na roz ki baatein zaroori, na har pal saath rehna. Bas kabhi-kabhi ek message, ek smile, ek "tu theek hai na?" Forever dosti ka raaz yahi hai ki hum perfect nahi hote, par phir bhi ek dusre ko apni priorities mein kahin na kahin rakhe rehte hain.

Admired in silence, Loved without permission. Intelligent enough to never pretend, Strong enough to walk away whole, Honest even when honesty bruises, Authentic in a world addicted to masks. In the feeling of her, I become the best shade of green— But she has always loved blue.
Anchored Restraint Preserving Inner Tranquility
A controlled life that chose quiet over chaos—leaving without noise, yet never without impact.
He lived contained, not because he felt less, but because he understood the cost of chaos. He chose peace with control over loud connection, and in leaving softly, he taught others how to stand without him. Woh logon ke beech hansi chhod jaata hai, par apni khamoshi kahin aur likh jaata hai. Zinda reh ke usne khud ko sambhaala, mar ke shayad apna bojh halka kar jaata hai. Usse uljhan se darr nahi tha, bas dil ke shor se bach ke chala. Kaam mein usne nishaan chhode, rishton mein bas itna ki — kisi ko tootna na pade. Woh dil deta tha, par poora nahi, kyunki usse yaadein bhaari lagti thi. Par jab woh nahi rahega, tab log samjhenge — uski kam baatein bhi wazan rakhti thi. Akela rehna uski kamzori nahi thi, yeh uska tarika tha zinda rehne ka. Par marne ke baad, uska sukoon logon ke yaadon mein badhega, kyunki woh kabhi bojh nahi bana. Woh chala jaayega bina hungame ke, jaise jeeya tha waise hi — shaant. Par jo ek baar uski zindagi mein aaya, uske andar ek aadat chhod jaayega: khud ko sambhaal ke jeene ki. Aur shayad yehi uski jeet hai — na roye hue log, na tootay hue dil, bas ek khamosh respect jo uske baad bhi zinda rahe. Kyunki kuch log jeete hain pyaar ke liye, aur kuch — taaki unke jaane ke baad bhi duniya thodi zyada stable lage.
Ek Nishani
Samjho to ehsaas hoon… warna sirf ek kahani.
Rakh sako to ek nishani hu mai, Kho do to sirf ek kahani hu mai. Rok na paaye jisko ye saari duniya, Wo ek bund aankh ka paani hu mai. Sabko pyaar dene ki aadat hai mujhe, Apni alag pehchaan banane ki aadat hai mujhe. Kitna bhi gehra zakhm de koi, Utna hi zyada muskurane ki aadat hai mujhe. Akela khwab hu mai, Sawalon se khafa chhota sa jawab hu mai. Jo samajh na sake — unke liye kaun? Jo jaan gaye — unke liye khuli kitaab hu mai. Aankhon se dekhoge to khush paoge, Dil se puchoge to dard ka sailaab hu mai. Agar rakh sako to ek nishani hu mai, Kho do to sirf ek kahani hu mai.
